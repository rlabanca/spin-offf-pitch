SPIN OFFFRisks
[ 19 ] What could go wrong, and how we hold it
Four honest risks.
Four concrete mitigations.
[ 01 ]
Free content online
We don't compete on volume. Curation, access, belonging — three layers free content can't deliver. Membership is a passport, not a paywall. (slide 20)
[ 02 ]
Pricing pushback
Below €12 forces compromise on artist pay or perks — both unacceptable. Early-adopter pricing at launch (M4) drives momentum without breaking the model. (slide 21)
[ 03 ]
Cannibalizing OFFF
Members get early-bird festival access; credits route spend into the OFFF marketplace; UGC feeds back into OFFF channels. Complement, not substitute. (slide 22)
[ 04 ]
Churn over 5%
Membership is multi-vector — content + perks + credits + access. A member who skips one vector still uses another. Diversification is the moat. (slide 23)

SPIN OFFFPep's main concern
[ 20 ] Pep's main concern
"How do we monetize when so much
creative content is free online?"
[ 01 ]
Curation
25 years of OFFF taste, applied weekly to one named artist. Volume is free; taste is rare.
[ 02 ]
Access
Physical events, early-bird tickets, mentor networking, marketplace credit. Free content can't deliver any of this.
[ 03 ]
Belonging
Members are participants, not consumers. UGC + collab section turn each member into a node in the OFFF ecosystem.
The membership isn't a paywall on content. It's a passport into the OFFF ecosystem.
