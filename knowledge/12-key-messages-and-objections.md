# 12 — Key Messages & Objections

The talking points the team uses everywhere — investor decks, sponsor pitches, artist outreach, press, social.

## The two key messages (lead with these)

### 1. Fight against samification
AI is collapsing creative variety into one homogenized look. SPIN OFFF curates — relentlessly, monthly, with one named artist at a time — so the people who care about visual culture have a place where the work *doesn't* all look the same.

### 2. Meaningful art and design to help fix this messy world
The world is loud and confused. Good design and meaningful art are how we slow people down, make them feel something, and remind them what creative work is for.

## Supporting messages

- **We don't monetize until we've earned trust.** Free newsletter for the first three months. No paywall before there's a relationship.
- **Artists need this too.** More visibility, more engagement, new revenue streams — built around them, not extractive of them.
- **Built on the OFFF community, not on ad spend.** 200K–400K creatives are reachable from day one.
- **DIY is back.** Members don't just consume — they build (UGC, the creative box, the yearly award).

## Objection bank

### Pep's main concern
> *"How do we compete and monetize when there's so much free creative content online?"*

**Response.** SPIN OFFF doesn't compete on volume of free content. It competes on three things free content can't deliver:
1. **OFFF-tier curation** — the same taste OFFF has earned for 25 years, applied weekly.
2. **Access** — physical events, early-bird festival tickets, mentor networking, marketplace credit.
3. **Belonging** — the collab section turns members into participants. UGC is **the great worth-state metric for OFFF events** — every active member becomes a node in the OFFF ecosystem.

The membership isn't a paywall on content. It's a **passport into the OFFF ecosystem.**

---

### "Why a newsletter? Aren't they a dying format?"
The opposite — newsletters are the highest-trust, highest-attention channel left. *It's Nice That*, *Dezeen*, *Motionographer* prove the model. Our advantage: we ship one curated artist per month, not a firehose.

---

### "Why would a sponsor pay you when you're new?"
Because they're not buying impressions — they're buying **adjacency to the OFFF brand and curation**. The sponsorship is integrated with the artist of the month, so the placement is editorial, not interruptive. We start at modest rates (€500 in M3) and scale with the audience.

---

### "Why €12 / month? Could it be cheaper?"
€12 / month is the price needed to fund the **ecosystem of perks** (sponsor toolkits, marketplace credit, event access), the **artist payment** (€2K/mo featured-artist fee), and the production team. Going below €12 forces compromises on artist pay or member benefits — both unacceptable.

Early-adopter pricing is allowed at launch (M4) to build initial conversion momentum.

---

### "Why launch April 2027 instead of sooner?"
The membership opens **one month before OFFF Barcelona 2027 (May 6–8)** so the festival itself becomes a member-acquisition and member-onboarding moment. Launching earlier loses that.

The newsletter starts **January 2027** because we need three months of trust-building before any paywall.

---

### "What if churn is higher than 5%?"
The membership is multi-vector: content + perks + credit + access. Members who don't read the newsletter still spend their credits and use early festival access. That diversification is the churn moat.

---

### "Why won't this just cannibalize OFFF?"
SPIN OFFF is **year-round oxygen** for the OFFF brand. It funnels members back into festival ticket sales (early-bird access), feeds the OFFF marketplace (member credits), and creates UGC OFFF can use. It's complement, not substitute.
