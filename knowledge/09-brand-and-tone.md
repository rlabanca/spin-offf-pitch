# 09 — Brand & Tone

## Naming

- **SPIN OFFF** — uppercase, single space, three F's. Always.
- Never `Spin Off`, `Spin-OFFF`, `SpinOFFF`, `Spin off`.
- Plays on: spin-off (literally a child of OFFF) + spin (the rotation, the cycle, the artist of the month rotation).

## Visual identity

- **Same DNA as OFFF.** SPIN OFFF is a clear sibling — not a separate brand world.
- The OFFF design system (color palette, typography, grid, logo treatment) **already exists in Canva / Claude design**. **Do not recreate it**; reference it.
- Each issue / asset adds the **featured artist's visual references** on top of the OFFF base — so every month feels distinct, but unmistakably OFFF-family.
- Color palette and typography customizations live inside the existing design system. Confirm with Pep before adding new core elements.

## Tone of voice

- **More inviting and friendly.** OFFF is curatorial and slightly distant; SPIN OFFF is warm, human, conversational.
- **FOMO is not allowed.** No "you're missing out", no "last chance", no countdowns. The brand promise is *we don't monetize until we've earned trust*; FOMO undermines that.
- **Community-first.** The reader is treated as a peer / co-creator, not a customer.
- **Earnest, not ironic.** We're sincerely trying to fix a messy world.

## Voice rules of thumb

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "We curated this for you because…" | "Don't miss out on…" |
| "Made by [Artist] this month" | "Limited time only!" |
| "Try this. If it doesn't click, no big deal." | "Last chance to subscribe!" |
| Cite the artist by name and credit them | Use generic "creators" / "talent" |
| Be specific (medium, tools, references) | Be vague or marketing-y |

## Editorial pillars

Every piece of content should serve at least one:

1. **Spotlight an artist** (with their voice, their references, their process).
2. **Fight samification** (show variety, taste, the unexpected).
3. **Build community** (UGC, member spotlights, collab calls).
4. **Connect to OFFF's legacy** (archives, previous speakers, festival moments).

## Languages

- **English** — primary, default for global audience.
- **Spanish, Catalan** — used as appropriate (Spain-based ops, Barcelona events).
- All three valid for internal docs and member-facing content.
