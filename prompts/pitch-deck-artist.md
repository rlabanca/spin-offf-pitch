# Prompt — Artist Pitch Deck (First Featured Artist)

## Role
Create the deck used to **convince an artist to become the first featured artist of SPIN OFFF** — to create the monthly concept, sit for the podcast, and co-promote.

## Audience
A working creative — designer, motion artist, illustrator, art director — likely already in OFFF's orbit (past speaker, alumna, community favorite). They have an audience of their own. They are protective of their time and brand.

## Outcome
The artist should say yes because:
1. *"This puts my work in front of the OFFF audience for a whole month."*
2. *"I'm being paid (€2,000), not asked to do this for exposure."*
3. *"I co-create the concept — I'm not just a logo on a page."*
4. *"Being the first carries narrative weight."*

## Tone
Earnest. Peer-to-peer. Not corporate. The deck is from creatives to a creative. Talk about the *work*, not the marketing funnel.

## Required slide spine (7 slides — keep it artist-respectful)

1. **Title + opening line** — `Be the first.` A direct, sincere invitation.
2. **What we're building** — SPIN OFFF in two sentences. The fight against samification.
3. **What "artist of the month" means** — you create the concept; we build a month of content around it. Newsletter, podcast, social, all centered on your voice.
4. **What you get** — €2,000 fee, OFFF audience exposure for a full month, podcast feature, co-promotion across SPIN OFFF + OFFF channels, your work in members' hands (and Y2+ in their creative box).
5. **What we ask of you** — one concept brief, one podcast interview (~1h), source material we can adapt for the newsletter, social co-promotion (your choice of intensity).
6. **The cadence (4 weeks)** — clear week-by-week so the artist knows the time commitment.
7. **The ask + first concept idea** — concrete invitation: *"Be the artist for [month]. Here's the rough concept we'd love to build with you."* Customize per artist.

## Constraints
- **No exposure-as-payment language.** The €2,000 fee is real and surfaced early (slide 4).
- **No template-feeling slides.** Each pitch should feel handmade for the specific artist — show their work, name their pieces.
- Visuals: lean heavily on the artist's own work in the deck. SPIN OFFF DNA is the frame, not the foreground.
- Tone: peer, not pitcher. Read it aloud — if it sounds like a marketing email, rewrite it.
- Cap word count per slide. The artist will scan, not read.

## Definition of done
- 7 slides, artist's own work featured throughout.
- The €2,000 fee is named explicitly.
- The first-concept idea on slide 7 is specific (not generic).
- A creative would forward it to a friend without cringing.
