# Spin OFFF — Market Opportunity & Financial Plan

> Original text supplied by the founder as context for presentation generation. Preserved verbatim.

Spin OFFF is a media company and new revenue stream for OFFF Barcelona — the world's leading creative culture festival. It follows a funnel model: website visitors → free newsletter → paid membership → (future) creative box subscription. The business is built around OFFF's existing community, artist relationships, and sponsor network.

## 1. Market Opportunity — TAM / SAM / SOM

### TAM — Total Addressable Market
~150–200 million people globally
The global audience interested in creativity, design, art, motion graphics, illustration, and visual culture. Includes:

- Creative professionals worldwide (~20M)
- Design and art students (~30M)
- Enthusiasts, hobbyists, and adjacent professionals (100M+)

Proxies: Adobe Creative Cloud has ~33M subscribers. Behance has ~30M registered users. The broader interest group — people who follow design content on social media, attend creative events, or consume design media — extends well beyond professional users.

### SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market
~15–20 million people globally
People specifically aligned with OFFF's world: professional designers, motion artists, creative directors, art directors, serious students, and digital artists who follow cutting-edge international creative culture. This is the audience of platforms like It's Nice That, Motionographer, Dribbble, and Dezeen — highly engaged, internationally minded, and willing to pay for quality content.
Defined not by geography, but by identity: people who see creativity as both a profession and a lifestyle.

### SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market
~500K reachable, 10,000–30,000 paying members (3-year horizon)
OFFF's existing community (past attendees, social followers, partner networks) gives Spin OFFF a direct launchpad of 200,000–400,000 people — reachable without paid acquisition.

| Horizon | Newsletter Subscribers | Paying Members | Est. MRR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | 5,000–10,000 | 300–1,000 | €3.6K–12K |
| Year 2 | 25,000–50,000 | 2,000–5,000 | €24K–60K |
| Year 3 | 75,000–100,000 | 8,000–15,000 | €96K–180K |

At €12/month average membership, 10,000 members = €1.44M ARR.

## 2. Launch Strategy

### Phase 1 — Newsletter (Months 1–3)
Goal: Build the audience before asking for money.

- Launch leveraging OFFF's existing community and social reach (unfair advantage)
- Weekly newsletter, free, featuring a curated artist of the month
- Artist paid to create the monthly concept — drives quality and co-promotion
- CTA in every issue: join the membership waitlist
- Instagram / TikTok content to drive top-of-funnel traffic

### Phase 2 — Membership Launch (Month 4)

- Open waitlist to paying members
- Membership benefits: free tools/discounts via OFFF sponsors, exclusive digital art, credit system for events and partner products, early access to tickets and events
- Early adopter pricing to drive initial conversion momentum

Core narrative: we don't monetize until we've earned trust.

## 7. Key Assumptions

- Membership price: €12/month
- Artist fee: €2,000/month (featured artist creates monthly concept)
- Newsletter → member conversion rate: 5–10%
- Monthly membership churn: <5%
- Newsletter sponsorship rates grow with subscriber base
- No paid acquisition in Year 1 — growth through OFFF's community and artist co-promotion
- Tax benefits from sponsoring art (OFFF's existing model with government and sponsors)
